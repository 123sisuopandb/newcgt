Who is Satoshi Nakamoto?

Satoshi Nakamoto is the pseudonymous creator of Bitcoin, who published the Bitcoin whitepaper in 2008 and launched the network in January 2009. Satoshi's true identity remains unknown, making it one of the greatest mysteries in technology history. After actively developing Bitcoin until 2010, Satoshi gradually disappeared from public communication.

What is the Patoshi Pattern?

The Patoshi Pattern is a distinctive mining signature discovered by researcher Sergio Demian Lerner in 2013. By analyzing the extra-nonce values in early Bitcoin blocks, Lerner identified a unique pattern that distinguished one miner's blocks from others. This miner, believed to be Satoshi, used a single machine with specific mining behavior, allowing researchers to identify approximately 22,000 blocks mined by this entity.

How Much Bitcoin Does Satoshi Own?

Based on the Patoshi Pattern analysis, Satoshi Nakamoto is estimated to have mined approximately 1.1 million BTC across ~22,000 blocks. Each block in 2009-2010 rewarded 50 BTC. At current prices, this makes Satoshi one of the wealthiest individuals on Earth - if they're still alive and have access to the private keys.

Why Haven't These Coins Moved?

With one minor exception (10 BTC sent to Hal Finney in January 2009), none of Satoshi's mining rewards have ever been spent. This ~15-year dormancy suggests several possibilities: Satoshi may have lost access to the private keys, passed away, or deliberately chose never to spend the coins to maintain Bitcoin's decentralization and avoid market disruption.

What Would Happen if Satoshi's Coins Moved?

Any movement from these addresses would be one of the most significant events in cryptocurrency history. It could cause massive market volatility, prove (or disprove) various theories about Satoshi's identity, and potentially raise questions about Bitcoin's foundational principles. Many monitoring services track these addresses 24/7.
